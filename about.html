About Design Errands

Design Errands is a series of design observations and essays about cognition by Roberto Casati and Goffredo Puccetti. "Design Errands", as a project and an approach, hopes to encourage the practice of observing our surrounding environment and paying attention to how things, systems, spaces, etc. are designed, and from that, probing into how design shapes the way we think and act.

Design Errands was originally in the form of a book; however, the pandemic has significantly hindered the publication process while also opened up opportunities for a digital reformation, hence this website. The website is designed by Van Anh Bui and made with HTML, CSS, Javascript, and p5.js. Focusing on the act of observing and recording instances of design in our surrounding environment, the website acts as a visual database of those observations and constantly stays open for contribution. Roberto Casati
Director of Institute Jean-Nicod in Paris, France

Roberto Casati works on theoretical problems related to cognitive artifacts and external representations, publishing on maps, pictograms, drawings, perspectival models, queues, music notation, and educational (in particular digital) tools, in the framework of an extension and generalization of the "two modes” account of reasoning, that is meant to be an alternative to "extended mind" theories. The main aim is to investigate the cognitive mechanics underlying the use of artifacts (shifting, bridging, recycling, contracting...) in a unitary framework centered on the tradeoffs between representational advantages. Pointing out a third mode (instrument assisted cognition) and a fourth mode (instrument delegated cognition), he has mainly pursued the study of instrumental navigation without delegation (pre-GPS wayfinding). A number of training- and field- projects have been realized.



Goffredo Puccetti
Assistant Professor of Practice of Visual Arts at NYU Abu Dhabi

Goffredo Puccetti is Assistant Professor of Practice at NYU Abu Dhabi. His area of expertise is in corporate identity and branding. His main interests are in the interactions of visual communications with policy-making processes. Prior to joining NYUAD in 2011, he worked in Italy, UK and France for international clients such as Axa, Louvre, Mars, NTT, Pirelli, Pyrex, Samick Music Corp. The first faculty of Visual Design at NYUAD, he has designed courses on topics such as Cross-cultural design, Calligraphy and Type Design, Brand identity; two classes of his, one on Creativity–designed to be taught at NYU Florence–and one on Wayfinding, are part of the Core Curriculum of NYU Abu Dhabi.



Van Anh Bui
Graphic Designer, based in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

Van Anh Bui graduated from NYU Abu Dhabi with a Bachelor of Arts in Visual Arts and she is currently working as a graphic designer in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. She also enjoys making works on and about the Internet.





A note on the type:

Title texts are set in Gothic A1 - a Korean and Latin font that is a versatile sans-serif typeface with multiple weights and optimized spatial distribution. Gothic A1 is designed by HanYang I&C Co.

Body texts are set in Libre Baskerville - a web font optimized for body text (typically 16px.) It is based on the American Type Founder's Baskerville from 1941, but it has a taller x-height, wider counters and a little less contrast, that allow it to work well for reading on-screen. Libre Baskerville is designed by Impallari Type.
